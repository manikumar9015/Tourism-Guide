Kadri Manjunath Temple

Standing tall amidst the bustling city of Mangalore, the Kudroli Gokarnatha Temple, also known as the Thousand Lights Temple, mesmerizes visitors with its architectural grandeur and spiritual significance. This Jain temple, dedicated to Lord Gommateshwara, the Parshvanatha Tirthankara (saintly teacher) in Jainism, is a true marvel of artistry and devotion. Whether you're a Jain adherent seeking spiritual solace or a curious traveler marveling at architectural brilliance, the Kudroli Gokarnatha Temple offers a captivating experience.

Step inside the temple complex, and prepare to be awestruck by its sheer scale and intricate details. The imposing entrance gate, or Gopura, adorned with colorful tiles and intricate carvings, sets the tone for the experience within. The main temple building itself is a sight to behold. Constructed from white concrete and adorned with exquisite glasswork, it reflects the architectural style known as Yakshayana. The most striking feature is the towering gopura, embellished with colorful tiles depicting mythological figures and floral motifs. Sunlight filtering through the colored glass panels creates a kaleidoscopic effect, adding to the temple's enchanting atmosphere.

Inside the main sanctum sanctorum, a colossal 51-feet tall statue of Lord Gommateshwara, carved from a single block of white marble, takes center stage. The intricate detailing of the statue, from the serene expression on Lord Gommateshwara's face to the flowing robes and the tiny figures of his disciples at the base, showcases the exceptional craftsmanship of Jain artisans. The serene ambiance within the sanctum, coupled with the towering presence of the statue, inspires feelings of peace and reverence. Around the main sanctum, you'll find numerous smaller shrines dedicated to other Jain deities, each adorned with beautiful carvings and paintings..
